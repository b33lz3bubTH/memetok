# NSFW Product Sales Playbook (B2B)

## 1) Core selling points to lead with

### Revenue-first positioning
- **Built for watch-time + ad inventory**: short-form feed mechanics increase session length, which increases ad impressions per user.
- **Owner keeps control of monetization**: ad slot ownership, placement control, and performance visibility for RPM/CPM decisions.
- **Team-based publishing workflow**: business owners can delegate uploads safely to staff accounts instead of sharing one root credential.

### Operations + scalability positioning
- **Low infrastructure cost**: designed to run on cost-efficient servers, making margins better in early growth stages.
- **High-concurrency media serving model**: optimized for large numbers of simultaneous viewers without enterprise-level infra complexity.
- **Fast posting cadence**: creator/uploader teams can keep content velocity high, which is critical for NSFW retention.

### Risk-control positioning (critical in NSFW)
- **Role-based access**: clear separation between owner/admin/editor/uploader roles.
- **Auditability**: track who uploaded/edited/deleted content to reduce internal misuse risk.
- **Moderation and compliance readiness**: make policy controls and moderation queues part of the pitch, not an afterthought.

## 2) How to convince clients (sales narrative)

### Use a simple 3-step value story in calls
1. **Current pain**: "You lose revenue when upload operations are chaotic, credentials are shared, and ad performance is invisible."
2. **Future state**: "Your team gets controlled publishing workflows, and your viewers get a feed designed for repeat sessions and monetizable watch time."
3. **Business outcome**: "More consistent posting + longer sessions + better ad control = higher monthly profit with lower ops friction."

### Discovery questions to ask every prospect
- How many people currently upload content?
- Do they share a single account or have separate user access?
- What are your monthly hosting costs and ad revenue today?
- Which ad formats currently monetize best for you?
- What is your takedown/moderation workflow and SLA?
- What is your biggest growth bottleneck: traffic, retention, upload throughput, or compliance?

### Objection-handling framework
- **"We can build this ourselves"**
  - Response: speed to revenue matters more than engineering novelty; this is deployable now and tuned for this exact use case.
- **"NSFW compliance risk is high"**
  - Response: yes, which is why access control, moderation workflow, and policy enforcement are included in process design.
- **"I’m unsure ROI is worth it"**
  - Response: run a 30-day pilot with baseline KPIs (session duration, daily uploads, fill rate, RPM) and evaluate on hard numbers.

### Proof strategy (what to show in demos)
- Owner dashboard and role permissions.
- Upload workflow by team member account.
- End-user feed responsiveness on mobile.
- Ad placement and reporting visibility.
- Basic moderation/takedown path.

## 3) Recommended pricing model for a small dev team

Use a **hybrid model**: one-time setup + monthly SaaS/support + optional rev-share upside.

### Suggested starter pricing (practical ranges)
- **Setup / onboarding fee**: **$2,000–$8,000**
  - Includes deployment, branding, role setup, analytics baseline, and go-live support.
- **Monthly platform + support fee**: **$300–$1,500/month**
  - Range depends on traffic volume, SLA expectations, and feature needs.
- **Optional premium tier**: **$2,000–$5,000/month**
  - For high-volume clients needing priority support, custom features, and advanced compliance workflows.
- **Optional rev-share add-on**: **3%–10% of ad revenue uplift** above agreed baseline.

### Packaging suggestion
- **Basic** (early operators): low monthly, capped support.
- **Growth** (most clients): moderate monthly, analytics + workflow support.
- **Scale** (serious publishers): higher monthly, priority queue, custom integrations.

## 4) Pricing logic you can explain confidently

Use this simple ROI logic in sales:
- If client currently makes `$X` monthly ad revenue,
- and your system improves monetization efficiency by even **10–20%**,
- the platform should be priced so client still keeps at least **5x–10x** your monthly fee in expected upside over time.

Example:
- Client earns $20,000/month.
- 15% uplift = +$3,000/month.
- A $900/month platform fee is easy to justify when net upside remains strong.

## 5) Minimum contract terms to protect your team

- **3-month minimum term** (avoid churn right after onboarding effort).
- **Setup fee non-refundable** once deployment starts.
- **Defined support SLA** by tier (response windows and channels).
- **Scope boundaries** (what counts as custom development).
- **Compliance responsibility split** documented in contract.

## 6) Metrics to sell outcomes instead of features

Track and report these monthly:
- Daily active viewers.
- Average session duration.
- Upload throughput per team member.
- Ad impressions per session.
- RPM/CPM trend.
- Takedown resolution time.

## 7) Practical go-to-market steps (next 30 days)

1. Build a short demo deck with problem → solution → ROI.
2. Offer a paid pilot for 1–2 anchor clients.
3. Capture a case study with before/after metrics.
4. Use case-study numbers to tighten pricing and raise rates for new clients.
5. Productize onboarding so setup becomes repeatable and faster.

## 8) Important legal/compliance note

For NSFW businesses, legal and platform-policy compliance can vary by jurisdiction.
Before scaling sales, ensure your contracts, moderation SOPs, age-gating approach, and data handling are reviewed by qualified legal counsel.
